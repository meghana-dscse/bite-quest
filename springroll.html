Fun Facts About Springrolls

Spring rolls originate from China and are often eaten during the Spring Festival, symbolizing prosperity. The crispy, golden exterior represents gold bars, bringing good fortune. They are enjoyed worldwide, with variations found in Vietnam, Thailand, and even fresh versions in Japan!

Ingredients:

  • Spring roll wrappers
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1/2 cup grated carrots
  • 1/2 cup cooked noodles (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp chili flakes
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  • Mix all the vegetables, soy sauce, chili flakes, and black pepper.
  • Place a spoonful of the mixture onto a spring roll wrapper and roll tightly.
  • Seal the edges with water or flour paste.
  • Heat oil in a pan and deep-fry until golden brown.
  • Drain excess oil on tissue paper and serve hot with sauce!
